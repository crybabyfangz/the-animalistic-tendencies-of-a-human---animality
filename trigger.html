TRIGGER

Trigger is the point where internal stability stops holding its baseline state and shifts into instinct-driven processing. It is not tied only to people or interaction, but to anything that disrupts structure, direction, or predictability.

This includes changes in routine, environmental instability, emotional pressure, loss of control, or sudden shifts in how a situation unfolds. It can be subtle or immediate, but the effect is the same: the system stops being passive.

Once triggered, response is no longer neutral. The system transitions into one of the animal states depending on internal configuration.
