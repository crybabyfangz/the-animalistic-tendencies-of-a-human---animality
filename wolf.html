WOLF

Internal Instinct

Like the wolf, instability creates immediate sharpening of attention. The situation is no longer experienced passively but becomes something actively tracked in real time. They begin mapping structure: what holds, what breaks, and what direction things are moving toward.

There is resistance to allowing disorder to continue without intervention. Even small shifts in control create internal pressure to reestablish structure.

External Behavior

They tend to intervene directly in the flow of a situation. This can appear as correction, direction-setting, or taking over parts of what is unfolding.

The core pattern is refusal to let instability continue unchecked.

Influence

Influence changes expression style but not intent. Control may appear strategic, emotional, or restrained, but the function remains restoration of structure.
